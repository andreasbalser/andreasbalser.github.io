User Centered Design Project

BuildYourOwnGottesdienst: Contextual Design Project from beginning to a clickable prototype

Project University Projekt in Group of 4
Task Especially, but not only during the corona pandemic, many people are not able to attend local church service and have no other choice but to reside to improvised online formats. The goal of our project was to create a solution that elevates the online church service experience
Duration 6 Months
Limitations Due to the corona pandemic, we had to execute the majority of the project online
Experiences User-Interviews, Affinity-Mapping, Visioning, Prototyping (on Papier & in Figma), User-Testing, ...

1. Understanding the User

Contextual Inquiry

In the beginning it was crucial to gain a good understanding of the current situation as well as the problems and traits of the user. That's why we conducted interviews with 5 Users of online church service and also accompanied them during the service to get a firsthand look at the situation. Interesting questions were for example:

  • How and in what context is online church service used?
  • Which formats are there?
  • What do people like about online church service?
  • Are there any problems or wishes?
  • …

After every Interview, we interpreted the newly gained insights and noted every single Insight on a PostIt-note (Affinity-Note).

Data Consolidation

To organise all accumulated information, we created an Affinity-Diagram. We took all previously written Affinity-Notes and grouped them by reoccurring topics and themes. These groups were then organised in larger groups until we had a hierarchical structure of 4 layers. As a whole, the Affinity-Diagram painted a comprehensive picture of the users problems and needs, which we could base our design decisions on. Alongside the Affinity-Diagram we also created multiple Contextual-Design-Models.

2. Generating Ideas

Wall-Walk

To generate concrete ideas for our problem, we conducted a Wall-Walk. We also included other people from outside the project-team to gain more unique perspectives on our data. We went through the Affinity-Diagram and all CD-Models and noted the most important and overarching insights as well as all ideas to address the users problems.

Visioning

As a next step, we formulated visions for the most promising ideas of the Wall-Walk in wich we visually told a story of how the life of a user would look like with a specific idea.

We then evaluated the visions and crafted one final, consolidated vision, that combines the advantages of all prior ones.

3. Definition of the Product

Prototyping

Due to the limited scale of our project, we decided on one aspect of our vision to create a paper prototype for.

With the "Gottesdienst-Baukasten", users can build their own service from individual modules and share it with others or watch as a group.

Testing

Next, we tested our paper prototype in two iterations with a total of 5 users. During the tests, the users should operate the prototype by themselves and try to explore its whole functionality. If problems occurred, we discussed them directly and sketched out possible design solutions together with the user. In that way we managed to improve our prototype in many aspects and also added useful functionality. Lastly, we implemented the final and improved prototype in Figma.
